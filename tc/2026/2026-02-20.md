## 2. Github Operations Decisions
* **Topic:** Adopt Dependabot for Dependency Management
    * **Discussion:** The committee discussed the redundancy of running both Trivy (via Superlinter) and Dependabot. It was agreed that Dependabot is the industry standard for vulnerability management.
    * **Decision:** Approved. Dependabot will be rolled out across all repositories. Trivy will be disabled in Superlinter configurations to avoid duplication.
    * **Context:** [Internal Ops Update]

* **Topic:** Consolidation of Project Health Files and Templates
    * **Discussion:** Nan Liu proposed moving common project health files (e.g., CONTRIBUTING.md, GOVERNANCE.md) and issue/PR templates from individual repositories to the central organizational repository. This ensures consistency and makes these files sharable across the entire organization.
    * **Decision:** Approved. Health files and templates will be centralized in the org repository, and redundant copies will be removed from individual repositories.
    * **Context:** [Operational Update]

## Review of open Working Draft PRs
* **Topic:** Breaking Change Annotation for Fulfillment ID PR
    * **Discussion:** A review of the PR allowing optional IDs in fulfillment revealed that moving a field from required to optional is backward-incompatible for the recipient (the merchant).
    * **Decision:** The committee agreed to retroactively apply a breaking change annotation via a follow-up PR.
    * **Context:** [PR #143](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/143)

* **Topic:** Standardization of Card Brand Strings
    * **Discussion:** Debated whether to enforce a strict regex/pattern (e.g., snake_case) for card brands or maintain an enum. Concerns were raised regarding internationalization (UTF-8) and the administrative overhead of a centralized list.
    * **Decision:** The TC will not enforce a strict pattern or enum at this time to maintain decentralization. Instead, "best practice" conventions will be added to the official Authoring Guide.
    * **Context:** [PR #187](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/187)

* **Topic:** Payment Instrument Display Ordering
    * **Discussion:** Discussed the ability for merchants to suggest a display order for payment instruments.
    * **Decision:** Agreed that any ordering provided by a merchant or payment handler shall be treated as a non-binding suggestion (hint) by the client agent, rather than a strict mandate.
    * **Context:** [PR #176](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/176)

## 3. Open Discussions & TC PR Reviews

* **Topic:** Loyalty and Attribution
