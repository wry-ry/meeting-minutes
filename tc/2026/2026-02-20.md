## 2. Github Operation Updates
* **Topic:** Adopt Dependabot for Dependency Management
    * **Discussion:** The Council discussed the redundancy of running both Trivy (via Superlinter) and Dependabot. It was agreed that Dependabot is the industry standard for vulnerability management.
    * **Decision:** Approved. Dependabot will be rolled out across all repositories. Trivy will be disabled in Superlinter configurations to avoid duplication.

* **Topic:** Consolidation of Project Health Files and Templates
    * **Discussion:** Nan Liu proposed moving common project health files (e.g., CONTRIBUTING.md, GOVERNANCE.md) and issue/PR templates from individual repositories to the central organizational repository.
    * **Decision:** Approved. Health files and templates will be centralized in the org repository, and redundant copies will be removed from individual repositories.

* **Topic:** UCP Schema Repository Ownership
    * **Discussion:**  Discussion on whether the schema repository should align with the "Domain Expert + Maintainer" approval model used for conformance tools.
    * **Decision:** For the short term, current maintainers will remain to ensure continuity while building Rust-based validator expertise.
 

## 3. Open Discussions & TC PR Reviews
* **Topic:** Breaking Change Annotation for Fulfillment ID PR
    * **Discussion:** A review of the PR allowing optional IDs in fulfillment revealed that moving a field from required to optional is backward-incompatible for the recipient (the merchant).
    * **Decision:** The council agreed to retroactively apply a breaking change annotation via a follow-up PR.
    * **Context:** [PR #143 Allow fulfillment method type (and id) to be optional on Fulfillment method updates
](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/143)

* **Topic:** Standardization of Card Brand Strings
    * **Discussion:** Debated whether to enforce a strict regex/pattern (e.g., snake_case) for card brands or maintain an enum. Concerns were raised regarding internationalization (UTF-8) and the administrative overhead of a centralized list.
    * **Decision:** The TC will not enforce a strict pattern or enum at this time to maintain decentralization. Instead, "best practice" conventions will be added to the official Authoring Guide.
    * **Context:** [PR #187 Add available_instruments to payment handler configurations](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/187)

* **Topic:** Catalog Capability for Product Discovery
    * **Discussion:** This PR introduces search and bulk lookup capabilities. It was noted that this is a foundational requirement and is ready to land in draft.
    * **Decision:** Approved to land #55 in draft to facilitate further iteration. Amit, Maxime Najim and Naga will provide a final sanity check by early next week.
    * **Context:** [PR #55 Catalog Search+Lookup capabilities for product discovery](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/55)

* **Topic:** Payment Instrument Display Ordering
    * **Discussion:** Discussed the ability for merchants to suggest a display order for payment instruments.
    * **Decision:** Agreed that any ordering provided by a merchant or payment handler shall be treated as a non-binding suggestion (hint) by the client agent, rather than a strict mandate. Drew and Daniel will take a closer look to review more implications of this change.
    * **Context:** [PR #176 Add optional display_order to payment handlers](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/176)

* **Topic:** Catalog Capability - Get Product Operation
    * **Notes:** This operation is identified as a foundational requirement for interactive Product Detail Page (PDP) loops. It allows for fetching specific product data based on selected variant states (size, color, etc.) without transferring massive variant permutation maps. A technical deep dive is scheduled for next week.
    * **Context:** [PR #195 get product operation for catalog.lookup](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/195)
