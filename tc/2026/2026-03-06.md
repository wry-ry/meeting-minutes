#### 2. Technical Decisions & Consensus

- **Topic:** Payment Instrument Qualifiers / Eligibility
  - **Discussion:** The goal is to provide benefits based on the selected
    payment instrument. The team discussed the challenges with attaching
    discounts exclusively to an instrument, as eligibility should also signal in
    cart or catalog views where an instrument is not typically selected. A
    context-level approach covers membership and loyalty scenarios across the
    entire buyer journey.
  - **Decision:** Conceptually agreed to model eligibility as an enhancement to
    the existing discount capability utilizing a context-level primitive. Ilya
    Grigorik and Alexander Schillinger will huddle separately to figure out the
    actual change. Ilya will also stage a separate PR to fix the discount
    capability schema for cart.
  - **Context:**
    [PR #214 feat: add payment instrument qualifiers](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/214).

- **Topic:** Increasing Pace of Progress & Working Sessions
  - **Discussion:** Acknowledged that the overall pace of progress feels slow
    due to long review cycles and individuals being spread thin. Standing
    working sessions could help the group swarm on complex issues and achieve
    faster alignment.
  - **Decision:** Approved the creation of a standing slot for a secondary
    working session each week, likely on a Monday or Friday.
  - **Context:** N/A

#### 3. Open Discussions & TC PR Reviews

- **Topic:** Line Item Disclosures
  - **Notes:** Maxime Najim brought up the need to handle item-level regulatory
    disclosures and disclaimers, such as California's Prop 65, allergens, or
    gift card scam warnings. The team discussed whether to model this using the
    existing `messages` construct or find a new format to support mandatory
    elements like image URLs. The group agreed to schedule a 30-minute follow-up
    session next week to jam on the details and catalog integration.
  - **Context:**
    [PR #222 Add line_items[].disclosures[]](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/222).

- **Topic:** Identity Linking & Delegated IDPs
  - **Notes:** Shopify is exploring ways to extend the identity linking
    infrastructure to enable accelerated flows for merchants utilizing
    third-party delegated Identity Providers (IDPs).

- **Topic:** Business Logic Error Response
  - **Notes:** This PR aims to modify the schema to allow an error response
    without requiring a resource ID (e.g., cart or checkout ID) when invalid
    information prevents the creation of the resource. Amit Handa will ping Jing
    to review and respond to the PR.
  - **Context:**
    [PR #216 feat(errors): business logic error response](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/216).

- **Topic:** Version Negotiation
  - **Notes:** Specifies how businesses and platforms negotiate protocol version
    compatibility, enabling the signaling of minimum and maximum supported
    versions and capabilities.
  - **Context:**
    [PR #200 fix: clarify version negotiation](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/200).

- **Topic:** Risk Signals for Authorization & Abuse
  - **Notes:** The technical primitive is considered necessary and the team
    prefers to add it to the protocol rather than via a side channel. Amit Handa
    is following up with Google's legal and privacy teams to clarify questions
    regarding the documentation language.
  - **Context:**
    [PR #203 feat: signals for authorization & abuse](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/203).

- **Topic:** Itemized Fees Extension
  - **Notes:** An external contributor submitted a PR based on the itemized fees
    issue. Maxime Najim will make an assessment of the existing PR to determine

  - **Context:**
    [PR #220 feat: Add itemized fee extension for checkout and cart](https://github.com/Universal-Commerce-Protocol/ucp/pull/220).
